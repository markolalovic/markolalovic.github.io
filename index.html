My research interests include computer science, applied mathematics, and statistics, with a focus on developing effective algorithms with provable guarantees. I am also interested in developing machine learning applications.
I completed my thesis at the Institute for Algorithms and Complexity for a MSc in Industrial Mathematics at the University of Hamburg. Before that, I was studying for a MSc in Applied Statistics and a BSc in Mathematics and Computer Science the University of Ljubljana.
I have worked on machine learning applications in biotechnology, medicine, and fraud prevention, building, evaluating, and deploying large-scale models. Previously, I was a student researcher at the Jozef Stefan Institute, where I was applying deep learning to robotics and evaluating machine learning algorithms.
